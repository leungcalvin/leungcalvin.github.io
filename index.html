Calvin Leung

As of the time of this writing, I am a National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate Fellow and a rising fifth-year PhD candidate at MIT in the Department of Physics. I am a member of the CHIME/FRB Collaboration, and am lucky to call Kiyo Masui my PhD supervisor. My research program centers on interferometric observations of fast radio bursts (FRBs) and developing cutting-edge techniques to localize them and use them as probes of cosmology, with particular emphasis on gravitational lensing. Nevertheless, I maintain very broad scientific interests. I am always happy to have conversations about quantum optics, the foundations of quantum mechanics, and new developments in atomic physics.
