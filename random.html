Completed

I've worked through these in some detail and would recommend them.
  1. Rachmaninoff - Sonata for Cello and Piano (what an epic journey!)
  2. Smetana - String Quartet #1 (I love this, especially the jolly second movement)
  3. Kodaly - Duo for Violin and Cello (This grew on me the more I played it)
  4. Tchaikovsky - Piano Trio #1 (Quite a marathon, and maybe my favorite chamber piece of all time. I don't know when I'll play it next.)
  5. Brahms - Piano Quartet #1 (I thought this was wild until I saw Schoenberg's orchestral arrangement of it. I especially love Mvt. 3)
    6. Mendelssohn - Octet #1 (No, I have not played Mvt 4, but I will someday.)
  6. Schumann - Piano Quintet #1 (Barely played this due to COVID-19)
  7. Schubert - Piano Trio #2 (This was probably the most difficult thing I've ever performed, maybe aside from the Shostakovich. The famous second movement is based on a haunting Finnish folk song)
  8. Shostakovich - Piano Trio #2 (I am still scared of the opening cello solo)
  9. Schumann - Piano Quartet #1 (second movement is the best)
  10. Beethoven - Piano Trio (Archduke) #7 (third movement is the best)
  11. Brahms - Piano Quintet #1 (The piano/cello/violin moment in Mvt. 2 is sublime)
  12. Mendelssohn - Piano Trio #2 (Buy a new C string for this)
  13. Martinu - Piano Trio #1 (weirdly catchy but anxiety inducing)
