Completed

I've worked through these in some detail and would recommend them.
  1. Rachmaninoff - Sonata for Cello and Piano (what an epic journey -- thanks to the indomitable William Wang for learning this!)
  2. Smetana - String Quartet #1 (the second movement is such a treat)
  3. Kodaly - Duo for Violin and Cello (this grew on me the more I played it)
  4. Tchaikovsky - Piano Trio #1 (quite a marathon, and maybe my favorite chamber piece of all time)
  5. Brahms - Piano Quartet #1 (of particular note is Schoenberg's colorful orchestral arrangement)
    6. Mendelssohn - Octet #1 (played this on the MBTA with 6 other people once)
  6. Schumann - Piano Quintet #1 (a COVID project? I guess?)
  7. Schubert - Piano Trio #2 (note the famous theme in the second movement, which is based on a haunting Finnish folk song)
  8. Shostakovich - Piano Trio #2 (still scared of the opening cello solo)
  9. Schumann - Piano Quartet #1 (second movement is the best)
  10. Beethoven - Piano Trio (Archduke) #7 (third movement is the best)
  11. Brahms - Piano Quintet #1 (The piano/cello/violin mini-trio in Mvt. 2 is sublime)
  12. Mendelssohn - Piano Trio #2 (Buy a new C string for this)
  13. Martinu - Piano Trio #1 (weirdly catchy but anxiety inducing)
